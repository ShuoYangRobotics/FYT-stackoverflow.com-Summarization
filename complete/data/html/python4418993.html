Since you already know how to program in other imperative languages I'd just start with the (Python Language Reference)[http://docs.python.org/reference/index.html]. If you come across a construct that you can't understand from the reference then find the corresponding section of the tutorial.

After that, sections 1 - 3 of the [Library Reference](http://docs.python.org/library/index.html as well as the sections on sys, string, re, math, itertools, os, and types to start with. After that, look at any other libraries you're particularly interested in.
